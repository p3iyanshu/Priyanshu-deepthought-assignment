# Part B — Methodology

## 1. How did you find these companies?
I followed a structured sourcing approach instead of relying on a single platform.
I started with targeted Google queries such as:
- medical device manufacturers Bangalore
- industrial IoT hardware companies India
This helped identify relevant segments and initial companies. I then expanded the list using LinkedIn company search and startup databases like Tracxn and Crunchbase.
Instead of collecting companies randomly, I created an initial pool of ~80–100 companies and then refined it through a systematic filtering process.

## 2. What sources did you use?
I used multiple sources to ensure coverage and reliability:
- Google search (for discovery and segmentation)
- LinkedIn (for company profiles and founder validation)
- Startup databases such as Tracxn and Crunchbase
- Industry directories like IndiaMART and TradeIndia
Using multiple sources helped avoid dependency on a single platform and improved accuracy.

## 3. What did your research process look like?
The research process followed a structured flow:
1. Created an initial list of companies from different sources
2. Removed duplicates and irrelevant entries (service companies, distributors)
3. Applied basic filters such as:
   - manufacturer
   - India-based
   - product-focused
4. Evaluated each company using a consistent framework:
   - C1: Manufacturer validation
   - C2: India presence
   - C3: Product differentiation
   - C4: Technical decision-maker
   - C5: Growing sector
   - C6: Growth signals
5. Selected only those companies that showed strong evidence across most criteria
This reduced the list to 25 relevant and high-quality companies.

## 4. What did you learn about the segment?
While working on this assignment, I observed that:
- Many companies position themselves as technology-driven, but only a subset actually build proprietary products
- Founder background strongly correlates with product depth
- Growth signals are visible through deployments and partnerships rather than public financial data
- There is a strong presence of emerging hardware-focused companies in healthcare and industrial segments
